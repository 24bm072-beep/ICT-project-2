Social Engineering

Social engineering is a method used by attackers to trick people into giving away information or access. It targets human behavior instead of computers.

What is Social Engineering?

Social engineering uses lies, trust, fear, or urgency to manipulate people. Attackers may pretend to be someone trustworthy like a bank, teacher, or IT staff.

Common Types

  • Phishing – fake emails or websites
  • Smishing – scam messages sent by SMS
  • Vishing – scam phone calls
  • Pretexting – fake stories to gain trust

Warning Signs

  • Messages that create urgency
  • Requests for passwords or codes
  • Suspicious links or attachments
  • Emails from unknown senders

How to Protect Yourself

  1. Do not share passwords
  2. Check email addresses carefully
  3. Do not click suspicious links
  4. Verify information before acting
  5. Report suspicious messages

Conclusion

Social engineering attacks can affect anyone. Staying aware and being careful online helps protect you from scams.
